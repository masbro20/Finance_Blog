About Me

Voracious Self-Educator, Financier, Want-to-be Father

First, I am a richly educated desert boy. I was born in Arizona, right between Phoenix on the north and Globe on the south. It was in a little village called Gilbert, where I grew up with my twin and little sister, along with three brothers. I also grew up with a desire for travel, history, and cultures-inspired mainly by my angel mother's instruction and pressure to learn everywhere and anywhere, from anyone and everyone.

Second, I am a economist. Hence, why I wrote this blog (or at least why I got it started). I was always fascinated by economics fromt the time I read "Whatever Happened to Penny Candy?" and became enraptured by the subject. After some fits and starts feeling I was the expert on all things economics, I left on a full-time volunteer trip for my Church to South Korea. After coming home, I was more than excited to get started on the major at Brigham Young University.

Third, I am a financier. After having travelled around the globe to Europe, Asia, and most parts of North America on service or other-related trips, I have seen my fair share of other cultures and economies. As such, I was drawn not only to economics but to central banking, private and public equities, and real estate. While I BYU, I explored various majors, including econ and accounting. But right in between, I found the queen of all business, finance.

If you're new to finance and need some help learning how the economy works, feel free to explore my blog for resources. I have taken time to make it simple and thorough-not an easy task for the holiest of all business subjects. (Okay, you may not feel my enthusiasm for the subject, but you can at least acknoledge it is up there with the greats.)
